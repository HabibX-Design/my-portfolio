A service landing page has one job: help the visitor decide whether the offer is relevant and worth contacting you about.

## Start with the outcome

The hero should say what the service does and who it helps. Avoid vague slogans. A visitor should understand the offer within a few seconds.

## Explain the path

Service buyers want to know what happens after they click. Show a simple process, what is included, how long it takes, and what they need to provide.

## Give enough proof

Use project examples, testimonials, before-and-after context, or industry-specific details. Proof does not need to be loud. It needs to be specific.

## Make the CTA obvious

The page should repeat the same core action in natural places: after the hero, after proof, and near the end. For most service businesses, that action is an inquiry, call, or project brief.
